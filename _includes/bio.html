Hi there!

I am researcher in Cryptography, working as a postdoctoral fellow at NTT Social Informatics Laboratories under the supervision of Masayuki Abe. In October 2022, I earned a Ph.D. in Computer Science from École Normale Supérieure. My thesis, entitled "Malleable Cryptography: Advances and Applications to Privacy-enhancing Technologies", was supervised by David Naccache and Pascal Lafourcade. I also earned an engineering degree (Ing.) in Computer Science from Universidad de la República, which I have obtained in 2018 (ranked 12/599). During my Ph.D. studies I was a CIFRE fellow working at be-ys Research. Prior to that, I was a teaching assistant (Ayudante, Gr. 1) at Universidad de la República, Uruguay from 2015 to 2018. My interests cover the design of provably secure and practical public-key cryptographic primitives and privacy-enhancing technologies. I am particularly interested in zero-knowledge proofs, anonymous credentials, and post-quantum cryptography. I am a member of the Latin Americans in Cryptography community and the International Association for Cryptologic Research (IACR).
