Hi there!

I am a PhD student at ENS-Paris working with the Information Security Group under the supervision of David Naccache and Pascal Lafourcade. I am also a research engineer at be-ys Research since 2018 and a teaching assistant (Ayudante, Gr. 1) at Universidad de la República, Uruguay, since 2015 (currently on leave). My interests cover the design of provably secure and practical public-key cryptographic primitives and privacy-enhancing protocols. I am particularly interested in zero-knowledge proofs, anonymous credentials and their applications in the blockchain domain. I hold an engineering degree (Ing.) in Computer Science from Universidad de la República, which I have obtained in 2018 (ranked 12/599). I am a member of the Latin Americans in Cryptography community.

My full (academic) CV is available upon request. You can find out more about me and my interests outside academia on this page.
